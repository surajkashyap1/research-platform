# Product

## Register

product

## Users

UK university students and early-career healthcare professionals (medical
students, junior doctors, nurses, allied health) trying to get their **first
research publication** — and the supervisors/professionals who post the
opportunities. The student side is often anxious and inexperienced: they don't
know how to find projects, what "an audit" involves, or whether they're good
enough to apply. The supervisor side wants reliable collaborators for audits,
reviews, posters, and studies. Two-sided marketplace; most screens are an
authenticated task (browse/filter listings, post a project, manage applications).

## Product Purpose

"The easiest way to get your first publication." Connect the two sides with low
friction: students find approachable, clearly-scoped opportunities (especially
beginner-friendly ones); supervisors list work and pick collaborators. Success =
a student finds and lands a real project, and a supervisor fills a position.

## Brand Personality

Scholarly, credible, encouraging. Three words: **authoritative, warm,
published.** It should feel like a respected academic institution that happens
to be welcoming to newcomers — the confidence of a university press, the
approachability of a good mentor. Voice is plain and specific, never hype.

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS / startup**: gradient heroes, identical feature-card grids,
  Inter/Geist everywhere, purple-blue gradients. The default AI-slop look.
- **Sterile hospital**: cold institutional blue-grey, "NHS waiting room."
- **The real PubMed / government database**: dense, utilitarian, 2005-era civic
  UI. We borrow academic-publishing *heritage*, not legacy-database *execution*.
- Not childish or playful — credibility matters to this audience.

## Design Principles

1. **Type and whitespace carry the design, not color.** Color is one restrained
   accent (claret) for actions and state; the page reads as "published," set in
   a serif display over a clean sans.
2. **Earned familiarity over novelty.** It's a task tool — standard affordances,
   consistent component vocabulary, the tool disappears into the job.
3. **Encourage the newcomer.** Empty states teach; beginner-friendly work is
   visibly surfaced; copy reassures without condescending.
4. **Credible by construction.** Verification, real names, and clarity signal
   trust; nothing decorative pretends to be meaningful.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum (anxious, broad, non-expert audience). All text pairs
verified ≥4.5:1 (large/UI ≥3:1). Visible focus rings, full keyboard nav,
`prefers-reduced-motion` honored, never color-only signalling. Light and dark
both first-class.
